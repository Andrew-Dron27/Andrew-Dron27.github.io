About me

Background, experience, and what drives me as an engineer.

I am 28 years old and graduated from the University of Utah in 2020 with a bachelor’s in computer science, with a specialization in systems engineering. I have 4 years of professional experience in tech: 3 years as an intern at Becton Dickinson and 1 year as a software engineer at Sorenson Communications. My work has focused on user-facing products ranging from ultrasound needle-tracking devices to web video services for sign language relay interpreters.

My strongest stack includes C#, JavaScript/TypeScript, React, Flutter, and C++. In my free time I study Rust because its compiler rules allow performance with strong safety guarantees.

Last year I resigned from my full-time role at Sorenson to pursue an adventure cycling across Norway with my cousin. We completed around 3 weeks of bikepacking, and I then spent two months backpacking through Europe. It was the adventure of a lifetime, and it strengthened teamwork and communication skills in ways that are hard to replicate in an office.

I am confident in my ability to contribute as a software engineer. I performed strongly academically while balancing internship work, and my travel experiences broadened my perspective and improved both technical and interpersonal problem-solving.
